## C) Teamwork and Communication

16. **Tell me about a conflict in a project and how you handled it.**
Answer: We had disagreement on feature scope vs timeline. I proposed a phased release: stable core first, advanced options in next iteration, and we aligned quickly.

17. **How do you communicate technical tradeoffs to non-technical people?**
Answer: I explain in terms of speed, risk, and user impact, not internal jargon. I give 2-3 options with recommendation and reason.

18. **Have you ever disagreed with a teammate's implementation approach?**
Answer: Yes, and I handled it through objective comparison: complexity, maintainability, and failure cases. We selected the simpler approach that met current requirement.

19. **How do you ensure smooth handover of your work?**
Answer: I keep handover notes with context, current status, known risks, and exact next steps. I also include API/test commands for quick continuation.

20. **How do you document decisions so others can continue easily?**
Answer: I write short decision notes: problem, options considered, chosen approach, and why. This reduces confusion in future refactors.

21. **What do you do when requirements change late in development?**
Answer: I re-evaluate scope and identify what can still ship safely. Then I communicate impact clearly and agree on revised milestones.

22. **How do you ask for help without blocking progress?**
Answer: I ask with clear context: expected behavior, current behavior, logs, and attempted fixes. Meanwhile I continue parallel work that is not blocked.
